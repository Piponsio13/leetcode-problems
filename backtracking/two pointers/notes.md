We use two pointers to efficiently find the maximum water area that can be contained between vertical lines. Starting with pointers at both ends of the array, we calculate the area formed between them (width × minimum height). We then move the pointer pointing to the shorter line inward, since moving the taller line would only decrease the width without potential for a taller minimum height. This greedy approach works because the maximum area must involve at least one of the tallest lines, and by always moving the shorter pointer, we explore all potentially larger areas in O(n) time.
